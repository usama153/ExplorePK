History of Northern Pakistan

Northern Pakistan, comprising Gilgit-Baltistan, Swat, and Chitral, has a rich historical legacy. Once part of the Silk Road, it has seen Greek, Buddhist, and Islamic influences. Swat was a Buddhist center, while Hunza and Baltistan flourished as independent princely states before integration into Pakistan. The unique blend of ancient traditions, languages, and landscapes makes this region a treasure trove of cultural heritage.
